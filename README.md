# Yite Labs — Brand and Design System

*EXP-002 · Design system · May 2026*

---

## What this is

The visual identity, colour system, type stack, and design tokens behind Yite Labs. Built before the website, because good things need a foundation.

---

## The process

### Naming

Started with Kite Studios. Moved to Kite Labs. Ended at Yite Labs — my nickname, a one-letter change from Kite, and the only name that felt specific enough to be ownable. Nobody else is called Yite anything. That's the point.

The brief that unlocked everything: a creative lab where serious work happens but the environment feels alive, irreverent, and genuinely fun to be in. Think Rick Rubin's studio. A good skate brand. A record label that takes the artwork as seriously as the music.

### The mark

Went through six directions before landing on the character kite — a diamond kite with a face, four colour panels, and a tail with coloured bows. The face was the right call. It's completely unique, impossible to forget, and matches the energy of the name before you've read a word.

The tail animates. Subtle wave, constant momentum.

### Colour system

Each pillar of Yite Labs has its own colour. Not decorative — functional. Every piece of content is colour-coded so the system does organisational work as well as visual work.

| Name | Hex | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| Ink | #1C0F08 | Anchor — midnight brown, not black |
| Ground | #FAF8F3 | Primary background — warm off-white |
| Ember | #E8501A | Experiments + tools |
| Moss | #1A7A4A | Products |
| Denim | #2D4FA8 | Thinking + writing |
| Lavender | #7B5EA7 | Work + advisory |
| Harvest | #C8A830 | Investing |
| Ash | #8A7260 | Body text |
| Chalk | #E4DDD0 | Borders, dividers |

The anchor colour (#1C0F08) is midnight brown, not pure black. The brown is felt more than seen — reads as near-black at a glance, surprises up close. More ownable than black. Warmer than navy.

### Typography

Three typefaces, each with a specific job:

- **Bebas Neue** — display only. Headlines, wordmark, section titles. Loud, confident, zero apology.
- **Space Grotesk** — body copy. Modern, warm, slightly irregular. Feels human at reading size.
- **Space Mono** — labels, tags, metadata, dates. Technical detail, stamps, serial numbers.

### The kite mark variants

The mark works on every background — light, dark, ember, moss, denim. The four colour panels shift slightly per ground but the personality stays constant.

At 16px (favicon) the face disappears but the four-panel colour blocking still makes it recognisable. At 32px the eyes are visible. At full size it has eyebrows, a smile, and cheek blush.

---

## What I learned

A design system built before the product is a forcing function for clarity. Every decision about colour, type, and spacing is also a decision about what the thing actually is and who it's for.

The name took longer than the logo. The logo took longer than the colour system. The colour system took about 20 minutes once the name and logo were right. That order matters.

"Serious enough to be trusted. Fun enough to actually be used." — the north star for every design decision. Still is.
